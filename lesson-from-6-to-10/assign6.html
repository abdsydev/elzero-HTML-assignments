Fiction Books

"To Kill a Mockingbird" by Harper Lee

A classic novel set in the American South during the 1930s, it explores themes of racial injustice, moral growth, and compassion through the eyes of a young girl named Scout Finch.

"1984" by George Orwell

A dystopian novel that presents a chilling vision of a totalitarian regime, where government surveillance and mind control are prevalent. It delves into themes of freedom, oppression, and the dangers of totalitarianism.

"The Great Gatsby" by F. Scott Fitzgerald

Set in the 1920s, this novel explores themes of love, wealth, and the American Dream. It follows the mysterious Jay Gatsby and his obsession with the beautiful Daisy Buchanan, highlighting the excesses and moral decay of the era.

Mystery Books

"The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo" by Stieg Larsson

This gripping mystery thriller follows journalist Mikael Blomkvist and hacker Lisbeth Salander as they investigate the disappearance of a wealthy industrialist's niece. The novel delves into dark family secrets, corruption, and the quest for justice.

"The Da Vinci Code" by Dan Brown

A fast-paced thriller that combines art, history, and religion, this novel follows symbologist Robert Langdon as he unravels a series of cryptic clues leading to a hidden secret that could change the course of history.

"Gone Girl" by Gillian Flynn

A psychological thriller about the mysterious disappearance of Amy Dunne and the ensuing investigation, which turns into a twisted tale of deception, betrayal, and unexpected revelations as suspicions fall on her husband, Nick Dunne.
